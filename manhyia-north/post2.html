General Manager for Lancaster Kumasi City Mr Basil F. Hamadeh has paid a working visit to the mayor of Kumasi Hon. Samuel Pyne .

The Ministry of Communications and Digitalisation (MoCD) has launched training for the second batch of 1,000 girls in the Ashanti Region as part of the “Girls-in-ICT” program. This training initiative, covering 47 districts, is organized in three batches. In the third week, training will extend to Oforikrom Municipal, Asokwa Municipal, Nhyiaso Sub Metro, Afigya Kwabre South, Afigya Kwabre North, Suame Municipal, Manhyia North Sub Metro, Manhyia South Sub Metro, Asokore Mampong Municipal, Kwabre East Municipal, Old Tafo Municipal, Subin Sub Metro, Bantama Sub Metro, and Atwima Nwabiagya North.Earlier, the General Manager for Lancaster Kumasi City Mr Basil F. Hamadeh has paid a working visit to the mayor of Kumasi Hon. Samuel Pyne . The visit was to strengthen ties between the two institutions. According to Mr Hamadeh the hotel has enjoyed support from KMA and this has yelled at mutual benefits for all. He stated that the Lancaster Hotel Kumasi City is committed to the society and for that matter pays good attention to their corporate social responsibilities by engaging and sponsoring requests that would inure to the benefits of all. The GM further indicated that the hotel has ventured into agriculture particularly vegetable farming so as to produce pure organic vegetables. This in house initiative is to supply fresh vegetables for food preparation for their clients. The Hon. Mayor on his part thanked the Lancaster hotel for being law abiding with regards to their prompt payments of property rates and BOP ( Business Operating Permits) to the KMA . He encouraged them to continue with their hospitality/ corporate social responsibilities to the city of Kumasi and beyond.
